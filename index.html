Still have Doubts?

We have answered some of the frequent questions for you!

At what point should I see a gynecologist?

You should see a Gynecologist if you have concerns about your menstrual cycle, pelvic pain, sexual health, fertility, or you want to plan pregnancy.

How many weeks pregnant should you see the gynecologist?

You should see a gynecologist as soon as you find out you're pregnant, ideally around 4-5 weeks.Early consultation helps in avoiding many critial situations. Early prenatal care helps monitor your health and the baby's development throughout the pregnancy.

Is it common to have irregular periods?

Yes, it’s common to have irregular periods, especially in the first few years after puberty or as you approach menopause. Factors like stress, weight changes, exercise, and hormonal imbalances can also cause irregular periods. However, if your periods are consistently irregular or you experience other symptoms, like heavy flow or less flow, it’s a good idea to see a gynecologist.

Are irregular periods normal?

Occasional irregular cycles are common, but persistent irregularity could indicate hormonal imbalance, PCOS, or other conditions.

Do irregular periods mean infertility?

Irregular periods don't necessarily mean infertility, but they can sometimes indicate underlying issues like hormonal imbalances or conditions like PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) that may affect fertility. If you're concerned about infertility, it’s a good idea to consult a gynecologist to identify the cause and explore treatment options.

Can women suffering from PCOS get pregnant?

Yes, women with PCOS can get pregnant, but they may face challenges due to irregular ovulation. With treatment regulate ovulation, many women with PCOS can successfully conceive

What is the test for infertility evaluation?

Infertility evaluation typically involves ovulation tests, hormone testing, semen analysis, ultrasound, and sometimes HSG or laparoscopy to assess reproductive health.

What is the best postpartum care?

The best postpartum care includes regular check-ups, monitoring physical and emotional health, breastfeeding support, and guidance on nutrition, exercise, and family planning. It's important to address any concerns early for a smooth recovery.
